Due to the social, political and cultural context of many places throughout the world, women are often disproportionately affected by disaster.[27] In settings where women and children are likely to remain at home, natural disasters, such as earthquakes, can result in greater morbidity and mortality among women. For example, during the 1993 earthquake in Maharastra, India, more women died than men as they were more likely to be in the home, due to their role as caregivers.In the 2004 Indian Ocean tsunami, more women died than men, partly due to the fact that fewer women knew how to swim
